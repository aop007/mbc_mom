Method of Moments (MoM) Core
============================

The ``mbc_mom`` engine solves the Electric Field Integral Equation (EFIE) in the frequency domain. For perfectly conducting wires, the total tangential electric field on the surface of the conductor must be zero:

.. math::

   [\vec{E}^{inc}(\vec{r}) + \vec{E}^{scat}(\vec{r})]_{tan} = 0

Where :math:`\vec{E}^{inc}` is the incident or source field, and :math:`\vec{E}^{scat}` is the scattered field generated by the induced surface currents.

Basis Functions & Impedance Matrix
----------------------------------

We discretize the unknown current distribution using overlapping basis functions, :math:`\vec{f}_n(\vec{r})`. The formulation applies Galerkin's method, testing the field with the same basis functions used for expansion, yielding the classical linear system:

.. math::

   [Z][I] = [V]

The elements of the impedance matrix :math:`[Z]` are calculated via double integration over the source and observation regions:

.. math::
 
   Z_{ij} = \frac{j \omega \mu}{4 \pi} \int_{L_i} \int_{L_j} \left[ \vec{f}_i(\vec{r}) \cdot \vec{f}_j(\vec{r}') - \frac{1}{k^2} \nabla \cdot \vec{f}_i(\vec{r}) \nabla' \cdot \vec{f}_j(\vec{r}') \right] \frac{e^{-jkR}}{R} dl' dl

Where :math:`R = \left| \vec{r} - \vec{r}' \right|` is the distance between the source and observation points, and :math:`k` is the wavenumber.

Multiradius Bridge-Currents (MBC)
---------------------------------

Standard piecewise-sinusoidal basis functions struggle when wires of different radii meet at a junction. The ``mbc_mom`` engine natively employs **Multiradius Bridge-Currents (MBC)**. 

When ``Mesh.build_dipoles()`` is called, the geometry builder automatically identifies junctions and constructs specialized overlapping dipole basis functions. This ensures exact continuity of current (Kirchhoff's Current Law) without unphysical charge accumulation at stepped-radius junctions, which is notoriously difficult to converge in legacy MoM solvers.
